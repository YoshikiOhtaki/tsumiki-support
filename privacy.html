Privacy Policy

We believe your baby's health information is private. Here's exactly how we handle it.

Summary

Last updated: July 2, 2026

Your data never leaves your device. No accounts. No ads. No cloud uploads. No data collection.

  • All symptom logs, photos, and videos are stored locally on your iPhone only
  • No account registration or sign-in required
  • No personal data is collected or transmitted to us or any third party
  • No advertising networks or analytics SDKs are included
  • AI features (where available) run entirely on-device using Apple's on-device models

Data Storage

Stored locally on your device

All data you enter in Baby Visit Notes — including baby profile information, symptom logs, notes, and visit summaries — is stored exclusively on your iPhone using Apple's SwiftData framework. It is never uploaded to any server.

  • Baby profile (name, birth date, feeding type, medications, allergies)
  • Symptom logs (temperature, feeding, diaper, cough, breathing, rash, etc.)
  • Free-text notes and visit summaries
  • Questions for the doctor

iCloud Backup

If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device, app data may be included in your personal iCloud backup — the same way any other app data is backed up. This backup is governed by Apple's iCloud Privacy Policy and is encrypted by Apple. Tsumiki has no access to your iCloud backup.

Photos & Videos

Photos and short videos you attach to symptom logs are stored only on your device. They are never transmitted to Tsumiki or any third-party service.

  • Photos are stored within the app's local storage
  • Videos are stored as local files on your device
  • Media is not uploaded, analyzed remotely, or shared without your explicit action
  • If you share a Visit Summary, only the text you choose to copy or export is shared — not the attached media files

To attach photos or videos, the app may request access to your device's camera or photo library. You can manage these permissions at any time in Settings → Baby Visit Notes.

AI Features

On-device processing only

Baby Visit Notes uses Apple's on-device Foundation Models framework to help organize and summarize your symptom notes. All AI processing happens entirely on your device — no data is sent to external servers, including Apple's or Tsumiki's.

  • AI-assisted visit summary generation runs locally on your iPhone
  • No symptom data, notes, or personal information is transmitted to any AI provider
  • AI features are unavailable if your device does not support on-device AI

Important: AI features in this app help organize and summarize your notes only. They do not diagnose conditions, assess severity, or provide medical advice. See the Medical Disclaimer below.

No Data Collection

Tsumiki does not collect, store, or process any personal data. Specifically:

  • No account registration or login is required
  • No analytics or crash-reporting SDKs are integrated
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA) are accessed
  • No third-party tracking or advertising frameworks are included
  • We do not receive any information about your use of the app

Medical Disclaimer

This app helps you organize notes for your child's doctor. It does not diagnose conditions or provide medical advice.

If your baby is having trouble breathing, has blue or gray lips, is unusually drowsy, is not feeding, or you are worried, seek medical care immediately.

Baby Visit Notes is not a substitute for professional medical care. Always follow the guidance of your pediatrician or other qualified healthcare provider.

Children's Privacy

Because all data is stored only on your device and no data is transmitted to or collected by Tsumiki, there is no personal information about your child in our possession. We do not knowingly collect data from or about children.

Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you via an in-app notice. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
